**Note: The time limit for this problem is 5s, 2.5 times the default. The memory limit for this problem is 512MB, twice the default.**

Bessie has recently taken an interest in magic and needs to collect mana for a very important spell. Bessie has NN (1≤N≤181≤N≤18) mana pools, the iith of which accumulates mimi mana per second (1≤mi≤1081≤mi≤108). The pools are linked by a collection of MM (0≤M≤N(N−1)0≤M≤N(N−1)) directed edges (ai,bi,ti)(ai,bi,ti), meaning that she can travel from aiai to bibi in titi seconds (1≤ai,bi≤N1≤ai,bi≤N, ai≠biai≠bi, 1≤ti≤1091≤ti≤109). Whenever Bessie is present at a pool, she can collect all the mana stored at that location, emptying it. At time 00, all mana pools are empty, and Bessie can select any pool to start at.

Answer QQ (1≤Q≤2⋅1051≤Q≤2⋅105) queries, each specified by two integers ss and ee (1≤s≤1091≤s≤109, 1≤e≤N1≤e≤N). For each query, determine the maximum amount of mana Bessie can collect in ss seconds if she must be at mana pool ee at the end of the ssth second.

INPUT FORMAT (input arrives from the terminal / stdin):

First line contains NN and MM.

Next line contains m1,m2,…,mNm1,m2,…,mN.

Next MM lines contain ai,bi,tiai,bi,ti. No ordered pair (ai,bi)(ai,bi) appears more than once in the input.

Next line contains QQ.

Next QQ lines contain two integers ss and ee.

OUTPUT FORMAT (print output to the terminal / stdout):

QQ lines, one for each query.

Second query: Bessie takes 50 mana from pool 2 after 5 seconds.

Third query: Bessie takes 100 mana from pool 1 after 100 seconds.

Fourth query: Bessie takes 90 mana from pool 1 after 90 seconds and 1000 mana from pool 2 after 100 seconds.
